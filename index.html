About

  • 1887

    Our Beginnings

    The parent company from which Marathon Petroleum Corporation originated is the Ohio Oil Company which was a collection of small oil companies that came together under one umbrella. In 1889 it was purchased by John D. Rockefeller's Standard Oil and remained under that name until the Standard Oil Trust was broken in 1911.

  • 1930

    Establishing the Brand

    The Ohio Oil Comapny bought the Transcontinental Oil Company and established the "Marathon" brand name

  • 1962

    Transition to the Brand

    The company officially changed its name to Marathon Oil Company

  • 2005

    Separation

    Marathon Oil announced a spin-off of its downstream, refining, and marketing assets to a separate company known today as Marathon Petroleum Corporation

Opportunities


Buisness Analyst

Do you enjoy working with customers and stakeholder to understand thier buisness needs? Do you like analyzing data? Are you able to take those things and translate them into technical requirements and solutions? As a Buisness Analyst, you will work with buisness partners (internal and external to MPC) and peers from other technical trsms to provide value-added technology solutions.

Infrastructure Engineer

Are you interestef in designing and supporting new and existing netwrok and server technologies? Do you aim to deliver connectivity to the business along with reliable end-user and application platforms? Do the challenges of cyber security excite you? As an Infrastructure Engineer you will explore the aress of networks, switches, firewalls, servers, data centers, and cybersecurity tooling.

Applications Developer

Is putting your coding process to work what you're lookng for? Do you want to engineer, dvelop, and implement software solutions based on buisness requirements? Do you enjoy testing and troubleshooting new and exisiting applications? As an Applications Developer, you will obtain the skills needed to build clean user interfsces, maintainable buisness objects, and learn how to properly interface with back-end databases. Additionally, you will experience aspects of the Buinsess Analyst role.
